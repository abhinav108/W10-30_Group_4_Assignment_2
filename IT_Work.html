IT Work


The IT industry is a large and dynamic industry that is constantly changing with time. It is important for us to gain an idea about the day-to-day life of being in the IT industry and what this industry demands from professionals involved in this dynamic environment. As it can be hard to get an accurate impression of the industry through secondary sources, we decided to directly speak to an experienced IT professional who is actively involved in the IT industry here in Melbourne, Australia. This IT professional is a Software Engineer in a major financial institution in Australia.

Our preferred method to do this was through a face to face or an online video interview. However, as the chosen individual was in a dynamic and busy industry such as the IT industry, this method was not possible. At the request of the interviewee, we decided to do an interview through email communication, as this allowed the interviewee to respond at a time that was convenient to him.

The chosen questions and the interviewee responses were as follows:

What kind of IT work does your job involves you in?

As a software engineer, I am involved in quite a few roles including but not limited to software feature upgrades and development, bug fixes, automation and unit testing, problem solving and brainstorming technical problems, Maintaining system health and upgrades, implement secure coding practices and guidelines, perform on call support on systems 24/7, fix production incidents, escalate risks and have a risk mindset, Database development, front end and back end development, working in an agile environment etc.

Please tell us about the industry you work in.

I work for an insurance company which provides insurance product and services to customers in Australia.

What other kinds of work do you have to do?

I am involved in mentoring junior developers, involvement in the recruitment process interviews, present dev talks to the dev community inside and outside of work, also keeping up to date with new programming languages and techniques via selfdevelopment methodologies.

Who are the different people you have to interact with in your day-to-day work? Please tell us about them.

Our team members which include various positions such as developers, testers, technical leads, scrum masters, product owners, engineering managers and colleagues outside of our team such as front facing staff, leadership managers etc.

Please tell us about your interactions with other IT professionals.

I attend technology meetups and conferences once in a while to update myself with latest technology advancements in the industry and it helps to meet new IT professionals from companies across Australia. But now a days it's mostly digital meetups and it helps to connect with developers across the world.

What about your interactions with your clients or investors?

I do not directly interact with clients or investors, but we work on features and developments which directly impacts company’s growth where clients and investors are benefitted at the end.

What aspects of your work do you spend most time on? Please tell us about these.

Most of my time is spent on implementing solutions to a given problem both technical and non-technical. I also see myself spend time on communicating with team members on technical requirements and provide advice on them.

Which aspects of your work do you find most challenging?

Getting the business requirements right from the beginning can be quite challenging as the business can provide different requirements with time which can then change the scope of work, thus change our delivery

Finally, can you share an example of the work you do that best captures the essence of the IT industry?

Recently I was involved with re designing of front facing application and it was a tremendous effort done by our team to deliver a better customer experience. This lead to reduce number of enquiries and customer were able to proceed with the flow successfully without confusion.

The responses from the interviewee were simpler than what we would have preferred, however, considering the busy schedule of an IT professional, these responses were highly appreciated by the whole team. We believe that they are adequate though to get an idea about working in the industry.

A main takeaway from the interview is that working in the IT industry means that you are not just involved in one job or task. You become involved in a broad range of tasks. Some that may require you to be on call 24 hours a day. You may also be involved in other tasks such as the recruitment process or mentoring of junior developers.

Another noticeable takeaway from the interview responses is that since the industry is a highly dynamic and constantly changing industry, it is important that an IT professional stays up to date with any technological advancements. As the interviewee has mentioned in his responses above, he attends technology meetups and conferences to update himself with the latest technological advancements in the industry and keeps up to date with new programming languages and techniques through the use of self-development methodologies.

The interviewee said that his daily interactions were mainly with his team members of various positions such as developers, testers, technical leads and scrum masters and colleagues from outside his team such as front facing staff and leadership managers. His interactions with other IT professionals were through meetups and conferences, that are now mainly digital meetups with developers across the world. The interviewee does not directly interact with clients or investors, however, has stressed that he works on the features and development that directly impact the company’s growth where the clients and investors are benefitted.

The interviewee responded that most of his time is spent on implementing solutions to a given technical or non-technical problem. He also said that he spends a lot of time of communicating with his other team members on technical requirements, while providing advice on them. The most challenging aspect of his work is seen to be getting the correct business requirements from the start itself. This is apparently due to the changing business requirements that in turn change the scope of work. This in turn changes the delivery.

The interviewees response to my final question, depicts the quality and the importance of the IT industry in this highly technological age. He has said that he was recently involved in the redesigning of a front facing application that the team put a tremendous effort on to enhance the customer experience. This redesign had reduced the number of enquiries and the customers were able to proceed with the workflow without any confusion. The impact of this redesigning on the customer experience and the resulting reduction of enquiries, shows how important this IT industry is in this age.
