Individuald Reflection

Abhinav Manohar

I enjoyed working as part of a team and have gained valuable experience in the process. We delegated roles and responsibilities to each member as a group, allowing everyone to work equally. Collaborative skills have improved as a result of working in groups. Working in a group gave me different perspectives and allowed me to tackle more complex problems than I could alone. We held regular meetings (2 formal meetups with all members and 3-4 informal meetups while working on the project) and used to prepare meeting agendas and minutes, which helped me learn how to plan ahead and manage my time effectively.
Our group had seven members, all of whom I found to be extremely supportive, which surprised me. We all have different time zones, which made it difficult for us to communicate at times. As a result, we devised a common time when everyone is off work. I took on the task of creating the group's website and expanded on the project idea. Along the way, I also picked up a new skill i.e. creating a 3D model to represent our concept. For meetings, announcements, and discussions, we used Microsoft Teams and Canvas, Google Docs for sharing our work, and github for sharing the group website.

Ayodhya Akalanka Kodagoda

Our group turned out be a very balanced group. Despite all these, I believe the team did great job together.

Mussab Syed

Working with the Food Tunnelz group was an interesting experience. Due to the coronavirus pandemic a couple of our group mates were not in Melbourne. Working on this project was an interesting experience as this was my first time working in a group with international members. We initially had issues setting up a meeting time, however once the project was on the way meeting times were mostly respected by all members of the group. Although I missed a couple of meetings, my group members were always understanding and willing to help me catch up. However sometimes, due to inherit problems with the chosen communication platform (MS Teams), unplanned communication was difficult. Due to the assignment requirements, it is not possible to move all communication to another platform, however it might be beneficial to move informal communication to a more suited platform. Towards the end of the assignment one of our group members left and some contributions from members were unsatisfactory. This left us with more work than expected. A lesson learned from these unfortunate circumstances would be to discuss potential problems beforehand, either through a group charter or an informal discussion, so that the course of action is laid out instead of last-minute discussions. Such obstacles have highlighted the importance of creating group charters in future group work.

Group Reflection

What went well?

We were able to manage and organise our group in such a way that everyone had an equal amount of workload to complete, and we prioritised our work accordingly so that everyone in the group could completed their share of work before deadline. We made sure that certain aspects of assignments were completed before certain dates so that we had ample time to fix any errors or issues before the due time.

What could be improved?

One thing that our group can improve upon is attending group meeting more often and communicating more often with our teammates. There were times where we had difficulty in conducting our meetings at the pre-planned time as some members were occupied with their work or were not at a place where they could appropriately join the meeting. Therefore, meetings had to be cancelled or postponed. This had a negative impact on the assignment and the timely preparation of it. Improving on that would mean that any work which hasn’t been completed by any of the team members can be completed timely, and so we don’t have to rush in any way before the deadline and submit our work in good quality.

What is one thing that was surprising?

A major surprise and a major setback on the group was when a member of the group withdrew from the course with only two days left for the assignment submission. He did send us his part; however, it lacked a lot of required information and contributed to added pressure on the team.

What is one thing that you have learned about groups?

One thing we learnt was that every member of the group is dedicated, smart and has their own strengths. Each member of the group contributed the required amount of work and in fact more than what was required to complete the assignment. This was despite of their busy work life or the unbelievable pandemic situation in India where two of our members are based in. We also learnt that working in groups is not as easy as you would think, as the situations that each of our members are in varied a lot.

Our GitHub logs does not represent how well our group performed on the assignment as we divided work equally with everyone. Our group decided that only one person will work on website and each of the other individuals will work on their part of assignment. This was done as that one person was highly knowledgeable in HTML site development. Instead of GitHub the minutes of the formal group meetings, messages and even informal group meeting on teams clearly represent how well the group worked on the assignment.
